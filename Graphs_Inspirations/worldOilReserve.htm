World_Oil_Production12.jpg1266x779 561 KB

This data visualization uses color as a mapping between boxes of variable width on the top and the geographical location of the countries. The width of the boxes maps to the size of crude oil production and boxes are connected to the map location of the country by a straight line. By using colors, it is much easier for the observer to pair the boxes with the corresponding countries on the map. Similar shades of color have only been used for countries that are geographically distant, hence avoiding any potential confusions.

The following info graph from NY Times portraying how the liberal and conservative wings of the Supreme Court voted in close decisions, clearly indicates the wings using red and blue backgrounds in the headshot of each justice.

In an instant we are able to tell that in six out of eight 6-3 decisions, the liberal wing voted as a bloc. We can also deduce that Justice Kennedy was one of two swing votes when the liberal wing decided as a bloc.
